Official Bio

Jihang Dai (b. 2000), a native of Beijing, China, is a Master of Art candidate in Music Theory Pedagogy at the Eastman School of Music, where he serves as graduate teaching assistant for music theory. Previously, he received a Master of Music degree in Music Composition from Syracuse University, where he studied with Dr. Loren Loiacono and Dr. Nicolas Scherzinger. He also holds a bachelor’s degree in mathematics and music from Franklin & Marshall College, where he studied composition with Dr. Julia Adams (née O’Toole).

As a composer, Dai’s work has been performed at the Atlantic Music Festival (Maine) and Brevard Music Center (North Carolina). His orchestral work Scherzo received honorary mention from the Brusa Foundation Award, and he has received the John Carbon Composition Contest Award from Franklin and Marshall College. He has collaborated with numerous artists. Some of his collaborative projects include a cello sonata for Syracuse cellist Jacob Bloom, an organ sonata for Oregonian organist Anne Spink, and Cazonetta for Russian violinist Dmitrii Tabala.

As a pianist, Dai has studied Dr. Jun Cho, Dr. Bonnie Choi, Dr. Mark Livshits, Ms. Elizabeth Keller, and Mr. Lü Xingzuo. He has performed at various venues including the auditorium at Beijing No. 27 Middle School, the Barshinger Center, the Phillips Museum in Lancaster PA, and the Setnor Auditorium in Syracuse NY. While studying at Franklin & Marshall College, he won the concerto competition twice, performing the first movement of Alexander Scriabin’s Piano Concerto in F-sharp minor, Op. 20 and the third movement of Dmitri Shostakovich’s Piano Concerto No. 2 in F major, Op. 102 with the F&M Philharmonia.
